Alumni Interviews: International Realities

November 22nd, 2025

Context: ENSEEIHT Alumni Day

During this event, I had the privilege of interviewing three senior engineers. My goal was to go beyond the "postcard image" and understand the cultural challenges of an international career.

Tech & Meritocracy

Yan Collendavelloo

Senior Engineering Manager at Meta (London)

The Path: France → Sydney ðŸ‡¦ðŸ‡º → London ðŸ‡¬ðŸ‡§

Key Takeaway: Yan highlighted the meritocracy of the Anglo-Saxon tech world. Unlike in France, where diploma prestige often dictates your career start, in London/US, "Respect is earned through competency and impact."

Cultural Reality Check: He warned me about the "Friendship Paradox". In the UK and Australia, it is very easy to become "mates" (going to the pub, chatting easily), but building the deep, loyal friendship typical of Mediterranean cultures takes much longer.

Rigor & Professionalism

Olivier Lallement

Head of Data Security at Richemont (Geneva)

Key Takeaway: As President of the Alumni Association, Olivier stressed that "Your network is your safety net when abroad." In the Luxury and Banking sectors, resources are huge, but the expectation for precision is absolute.

Cultural Reality Check: He discussed the concept of "Professional Distance". In Swiss/Germanic work cultures, the separation between private and professional life is strict. Punctuality is not just politeness; it is the highest form of respect.
